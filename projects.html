Structural and Thermal Evaluation of Electronic Power Conditioner (EPC) Unit for Space Payload

Structural Analysis Thermal Analysis Topology Optimization

Structural and Thermal Evaluation of Electronic Power Conditioner (EPC) Unit for Space Payload

  • Designed three iterations of an Electronic Power Conditioner (EPC) unit for microwave space payloads using Autodesk Inventor
  • Conducted comprehensive structural and thermal simulations, including modal, quasi-static, random response, steady-state, and transient thermal analyses, using Ansys Workbench to evaluate design performance under space conditions
  • Performed DFMEA to identify potential failure modes in the EPC design, enabling proactive mitigation of structural and thermal risks.
  • Performed topology optimization to minimize material usage while maintaining structural integrity, resulting in reduction of weight by 47%
  • Performed all the analysis with the combination of 1-D, 2-D and 3-D elements for different parts of the EPC unit.

Simulation of crack behavior of secondary particles in Li-ion battery electrodes during lithiation/de-lithiation cycles

Simulating crack formation in Li-ion battery electrodes during charge cycles by modeling particle structures with MATLAB and analyzing diffusion and cohesion in COMSOL.

Battery Simulation COMSOL MATLAB

Simulation of crack behavior of secondary particles in Li-ion battery electrodes during lithiation/de-lithiation cycles

  • Developed Voronoi Algorithm to generate Secondary Particle consisting of Primary Particle in Battery using MATLAB
  • Performed Diffusion Analysis in COMSOL to get the diffusion rate of Li ion.
  • Based on the output of Diffusion analysis, the Maximum Tensile stress was simulated.
  • Developing cohesive bonding between the primary particles to obtain the information of the cracks developed using COMSOL.
